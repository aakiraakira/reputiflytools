5) Revisions policy and workflow

Your revision definition, one-list rule, and no drip-feeding

A Revision is defined as one consolidated round of changes provided in a single, written feedback list within the original project scope. Any items from that list missed by our team will be corrected at no cost if flagged within 48 business hours of delivery. Any late additions, new requests, or tasks requiring complex custom code/new functionality will be quoted separately or billed as a subsequent revision at our discretion.

One list, one round: A Revision Round must be submitted as a single consolidated feedback list. The Client is responsible for consolidating internal stakeholder feedback before submission.

Official channel: Revisions must be submitted through the agreed written workflow (for example, the project revision document, or the Scope tool if it supports revision capture). No drip-feeding after submission: after the Client confirms a Revision Round is submitted and ready for implementation, the Client must not add, edit, or append new items to the same round. Any additional items become a subsequent Revision Round or a Change Request (and may count against included rounds).

Included Revision Rounds (if any) are stated in the project document. Once included rounds are used, further revisions require a new quotation or an additional revision fee.
