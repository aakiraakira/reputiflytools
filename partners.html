Image Handling Protocol

The Logic: Why are we doing this?

Gemini is a text-based AI. It cannot "look" at files on your desktop or inside a folder.
To display an image, Gemini needs a direct internet link (URL) to that image.

Our goal in this step is simple: Get the URL so Gemini can write the code.

Scenario A: Client Sent Image Files

The file is currently trapped on your computer. We need to upload it to the internet (WordPress) to generate a link for Gemini.

1
Go to Media → Add New. Upload the file.
2
Click the "Copy URL to clipboard" button next to the image.
Outcome

You now have a URL to give to Gemini.

Scenario B: Image is Online

The image is already on the internet (e.g., Google Images, Client's Old Site). We just need to "steal" the link for Gemini.

1
Right-Click the image directly in your browser.
2
Select "Copy Image Address" (or Copy Image Link).
Outcome

You now have a URL to give to Gemini.

What to tell Gemini (For Both Scenarios)

Since both methods result in a URL, the instruction to the AI is exactly the same.

"Use this image for the section background: [PASTE URL HERE]"
