Optimization

Commenting Structure

To maximize speed, our developers need Precision, not vibes. Vague comments lead to multiple revision rounds, which slows down your project.

‚ùå The "Vibe" Approach (Slow)

"Make it pop."

Why it fails: "Pop" means nothing. We will guess, and you might hate it.

"Make it amazing."

Why it fails: Subjective. We cannot code "amazing."

"Give it more pizzazz."

Why it fails: This usually leads to 3 extra days of back-and-forth emails.

‚úÖ The Precision Approach (Fast)

"Increase the logo size by 20%."

Why it works: Relative math is easy for us to execute perfectly.

"Make the headline bolder and change text to 'Welcome Home'."

Why it works: Specific instruction = Instant fix.

"Match the button color to the blue in our logo."

Why it works: Clear visual reference.

Google Images (The Right Way)

The fastest way to get exactly what you want.

  1. Search for your topic (e.g., "Modern Office").
  2. Click Tools → Size → Large (Critical!).
  3. Click the image to expand it.
  4. Right Click → Copy Image.
  5. Paste directly into the Green Zone.
