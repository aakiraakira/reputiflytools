    Let's get your ads set up

    You do this once. Roughly 40 minutes of clicking, plus a few days of waiting for Facebook to check your company.

    Use a laptop or desktop

    Not your phone. Facebook hides half of these settings on mobile, and one choice below can never be undone.

    Have these ready

    • Your ACRA business profile (PDF). Ask your accountant to email it to you before you start.
    • A recent utility bill. Facebook sometimes asks for it to confirm your address.
    • Your NRIC. One boss photographs it, front and back, during the verification step.
    • A credit card, and your phone beside you. Your bank sends a code to approve the first charge.
    • Your own Facebook login. Not your spouse's, not your staff's.
    • The phone that runs your business WhatsApp. The number customers already message you on.

    Step 1 ~1 min

    Where are you starting from?

    Two questions. They decide which version of the Page step you get.

    Can you log into a Facebook account right now?

    Open your business on Facebook. What does the big button say?

    Can you post as the business, and do you see a Professional dashboard?

    Step 2 ~5 min

    Your Facebook account

    Facebook requires a real personal account behind every business. Nobody ever sees it. Only your company name is shown publicly.

    facebook.com Turn on 2-step login
    1. Create the account with your real name and a personal email.
    2. Check the name on your profile matches your NRIC.
    3. Turn on two-step login so you never get locked out.
    Your Facebook name can only be changed once every 60 days

    If your profile says "Jane Tan" but your NRIC says "Tan Xiao Mei", fix it now and get it right first time. Facebook checks this later and a mismatch blocks your ads.

    If Facebook asks for your NRIC (front and back) or a selfie video

    Normal, and genuinely Facebook. It happens on new or long-dormant accounts. Take the photo and carry on.

    You should now see: your own name and photo at the top-right of Facebook, and two-step login switched on.

    Step 3 ~5 min

    Your Facebook Page

    Ads run from a Page, never from a personal profile. Here is how to tell them apart.

    A Page. Counts followers. Button says Follow. Has a Professional dashboard.

    A personal profile. Counts friends. Buttons say Add to story and Edit profile. This cannot run ads.

    You are fine

    Your Page is a real Page and you control it. Nothing to do here. Continue.

    Someone else owns your Page

    Message them once and ask to be made an admin. It takes them two minutes.

    If they do not reply, the formal recovery route through Meta takes weeks and often fails. Tell us. In most cases we simply build you a new Page. Losing a Page with a few dozen followers costs you nothing.

    If an old agency's business account owns your Page

    Being made an admin is not enough. A Page can only live inside one business account. They have to release it from theirs, or we build you a new Page.

    What you have is a personal profile, not a Page

    Meta removed the tool that converted one into the other. You are starting a new Page from zero: posts, photos, reviews and followers do not come across.

    It is annoying and it is still the right move. A profile cannot run ads and cannot carry a WhatsApp button, and Facebook can delete it for pretending to be a business. Do not delete the old profile. Just leave it.

    Cleanest possible start

    Nothing to untangle. Create the Page, add your logo and a cover photo. That is enough to run ads.

    More than one Page for your business?

    It happens when two people each made one. Tell us which is the real one. Do not try to merge them yourself.

    Create a Page

    Use your real trading name and pick the category that matches your business.

    Then publish it

    A brand-new Page can sit unpublished. Open the Page settings and make sure it is published and visible to everyone. A hidden Page cannot run ads.

    You should now have: a real Page for your business — it says Follow and counts followers. If yours says Add friend, it is a profile, not a Page. Reply to us and we will make you one.

    Step 4 ~10 min

    Your business account

    Meta calls it a Business Portfolio. It is a free private folder holding your Page, your ads and your card.

    business.facebook.com
    1. Click Create a business portfolio. Fill in your trading name, your name, and an email you can open right now.
    2. Check that inbox and click Facebook's confirmation link. Look in Spam.
    3. Go to Settings → Business info and paste your legal company name and registered address, copied straight from your ACRA PDF.
    Copy and paste. Never type it from memory.

    Facebook checks this against government records. Drop a full stop in PTE. LTD., or use your shop name instead of your company name, and it gets rejected. Each rejection costs days.

    Already have one? Do not make another.

    If a previous agency set one up, use it. Open Settings → Business info and check the company details match your ACRA document. If it is already verified, that is a head start.

    Otherwise create exactly one

    Meta limits how many one person can make. If it errors, stop and reply to us here. Do not click Create again.

    You should now see: your business account open, with your legal company name and address filled in under Business info.

    Step 5 ~2 min

    Put your Page inside your business account

    The step everybody skips, and the one that quietly breaks everything at the end.

    Why this matters

    A Page you made on Facebook belongs to you personally, not to the business account you just created. Later, when you share things with us, Facebook only offers the assets your business account owns. If the Page is not in there, it will not appear in the list, and we will never be able to run your ads.

    Have an Instagram for the business?

    You should now see: your Page listed under Pages inside your business account. If it is not there, it did not get added. Try again.

    Step 7 ~10 min, then a few days

    Confirm who pays for the ad

    Singapore law. Every advertiser must say who pays for an ad and who benefits. You cannot advertise here without it.

    Do this the moment the ad account exists, before anything else

    It is the only part with a waiting period, and it cannot start any earlier because it lives inside Ads Manager. Submit it now, then carry on with the card and WhatsApp while Meta reviews it. Leaving it until last wastes a week.

    Open Ads Manager

    What to do

    1. Open Ads Manager (or click the Singapore verification banner when Facebook shows it).
    2. Go to the menu, then Ad account settings.
    3. Find Verifications for ads targeting Singapore, and start it.
    4. Enter your company name, UEN and address — exactly as on your ACRA PDF.
    5. Upload your ACRA PDF. If it asks for address proof, upload your utility bill.
    6. One boss photographs their NRIC, front and back.
    The exact screens appear once your ad account exists

    Follow Facebook's prompts. They match the six steps above. If a screen looks different, reply to us here with a screenshot.

    You will be asked to photograph your NRIC, front and back

    Not just the company documents. One boss confirms their identity this way (passport also works). It is genuine, it is required by Singapore law, and it is not a scam.

    This is the step people fail

    Almost always because the company name does not match ACRA. Copy and paste from the PDF. Never type it.

    Your company name becomes public

    Once the ad runs, your Pte Ltd name shows on it and in Facebook's public ad library. Your personal name and address do not. Every Singapore advertiser has this. Ours reads REPUTIFLY PTE. LTD.

    You should now see: a "submitted" or "in review" message. It can take a few days. Do not wait for it — carry on with the next steps.

    Step 8 ~5 min

    Your payment card

    Wait about ten minutes after creating the ad account before you do this.

    Do not create the account and add the card within the same few minutes

    New account, new card, straight to spending. That pattern looks like fraud to Meta and it can freeze the account on day one.

    Billing & payments
    • Use a personal credit card if you can. Company debit cards are declined far more often.
    • Your bank sends an approval code. Have your phone in your hand. Miss it and the card fails.
    • A small temporary charge, about a dollar, may appear. It reverses itself.
    Meta charges you, not us

    The card sits on your account in your name. Reputifly never holds, moves or bills your ad money. Our fee is separate.

    Expect a low limit at first

    Meta caps a brand-new ad account at roughly $25 to $50 a day. It rises on its own over the first weeks. Your first month will look slower than your third.

    You should now see: your card listed, and the account no longer saying "no payment method".

    Step 9 ~10 min

    Connect WhatsApp

    Our ads open one button, and it goes to WhatsApp. Your business WhatsApp number gets connected to your Page.

    Use your existing WhatsApp Business number

    The number your customers already message you on. Nothing about your chats or your phone changes.

    1. On your computer, open your Page.
    2. Go to Settings → Linked accounts → WhatsApp.
    3. Click Connect and enter your business WhatsApp number.
    4. A code arrives on that phone. Type it in.
    If it asks which business to attach WhatsApp to

    Choose the business account you made earlier, the same one holding your Page and ad account. This cannot be changed afterwards. The wrong one leaves the connection stuck on "pending" forever.

    Only have WhatsApp on your personal number?

    Get a separate number first — even a $10 prepaid SIM — and set up WhatsApp Business on it. Moving your personal number in would log you out of your personal chats, and one number can only serve one Page.

    If it says the number is already in use

    It is connected to another Page somewhere. Stop and reply to us here. Do not keep trying.

    You should now see: WhatsApp shown as Connected in your Page settings.
