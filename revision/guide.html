Client Operations Manual

This document is your Source of Truth. We utilize a rapid-deployment Revision Portal that feeds your inputs directly into our engineering pipeline for instant execution.

The Golden Rules of Submission
  • 1. No Off-Platform Feedback: All revisions must go through the portal. We do not accept changes via WhatsApp, loose emails, marked-up PDFs, or phone calls. Any feedback sent outside the portal will be politely redirected here.
  • 2. One Issue Per Upload: Do not upload a single screenshot and write 15 completely unrelated bullet points underneath it. Keep your uploads and comments strictly tied to one specific fix.
  • 3. Precision over Vibes: "Make it pop" is not an instruction. Our developers need specific layout, color, and text directives to build exactly what you want.

Why do we do this? We want to get your website built precisely and launched as fast as possible. We have spent years developing and refining this exact system to eliminate miscommunications, endless email chains, and delayed launches. Following these rules ensures your project stays on the absolute fastest track to completion.

Orientation

Understanding Your Workspace

The Revision Portal is designed to mirror your website exactly. It is broken down into simple, manageable pieces so you always know exactly where you are leaving feedback. Here is an overview of the interface:

Reputifly Revision Portal

The Left Sidebar: Pages

On the left side of your screen, you will see a list of tabs representing your website's pages (e.g., "Home Page", "Contact Us"). You must click through each tab to view the entire scope of your project and ensure you haven't missed any pages.

The Main Area: Sections

When you click a page, the middle of your screen populates with blocks corresponding to the sections of that page (e.g., "Hero Section", "Testimonials"). Each section contains its own dashed Drop-Zone for images and a text box for general comments.

Data Structuring

Uploading & Annotating

To submit visual feedback, simply Drag and Drop or Paste (Ctrl+V) images directly into the dashed upload zone for that specific section.

The Built-in Annotation Canvas

Do not try to explain "the button on the bottom left corner next to the picture". Once your image finishes uploading, click directly on the image card. This will open our built-in drawing tool. Use your mouse to draw a bright red circle around exactly what needs fixing, then hit Save!

The Golden Rule: Select Your Upload Type

It is incredibly straightforward: every single image you upload falls into one of two categories. You must select one from the dropdown menu, or the system will block your submission. There is no in-between.

Type 1: "Fix" (Reference)

What it is: A screenshot you took of the site to point out an issue. We look at this to understand your request, but we don't code the image itself into the website.

Global Scope

Page-Wide Directives

Next to the page title at the top of the screen, you will find a button for Page-Wide Directives. This is an optional tool used to apply a single instruction to every single section on that page simultaneously.

Page-Wide Directives
Do This

When to use it

Use this for universal rules that apply globally. You can use the quick-inject buttons to help format these instructions.

" Change all primary buttons to #FF0000 "
" Capitalize all H2 headers across the page. "
" Please change all instances of the WhatsApp Number. "
Don't Do This

When NOT to use it

Do not use this for section-specific changes or vague redesign feedback.

" Change the image in the hero section. "
" Make the whole page look cleaner. "
Note: 95% of revisions do not require page-wide rules. It is perfectly fine to leave this empty.
Critical Restraints

Text Changes & Large Media

Do not drag Videos or Documents into the image drop zone.

To keep our compiling system lightning-fast, the drag-and-drop zones are configured to strictly accept images only (.png, .jpg, .webp). If you attempt to drag a 50MB MP4 or a PDF into the zone, the system will ignore it to prevent crashing your browser.

The Solution: The "General Instructions" Box

Every section in the portal has a large text area called "General Instructions" located underneath the image upload zone. You must use this box for pure text rewrites and providing links for large media files.

Scenario 1: Rewriting a Paragraph

You don't need to take a screenshot just to change a sentence! Leave the image drop-zone completely empty. Go straight to the General Instructions text box and type:
"Please replace the second paragraph with the following text: [Insert your exact new text here]."

Scenario 2: Large Background Video

⚠️ Not Recommended: Large background videos negatively impact your website's SEO rankings and page load speeds. Only request this if absolutely necessary for your brand.

If you still want a looping video, upload your .mp4 to Google Drive or unlisted YouTube. In the General Instructions box, type:
"Please set the background to play this video: [Insert Google Drive Link. Ensure permissions are set to 'Anyone with link can view']."

Scenario 3: Downloadable PDF Form

You want a button that allows users to download a PDF menu. Upload the PDF to Google Drive. In the General Instructions box, type:
"Link the 'Download Menu' button to this PDF: [Insert Google Drive Link here]."

Scenario 4: General Section Directives

Sometimes you have feedback that applies to the entire section, like spacing, colors, or ordering. Don't use a screenshot for this. Just use the General Instructions box:
"Please change the background color of this entire section to black (#000000) and ensure all text inside it is changed to white."

Optimization

Precision Commenting

To maximize speed, our developers need Precision, not vibes. Vague comments lead to multiple revision rounds, which severely slows down your project.

❌ The "Vibe" Approach (Slow)

"Make it pop."

Why it fails: "Pop" means nothing. We will guess, and you might hate it.

"I don't really like this section."

Why it fails: We don't know *what* you don't like. The color? The text? The layout?

"Change the text to make it sound better."

Why it fails: We are developers, not your brand copywriters. We need the exact words you want.

✅ The Precision Approach (Fast)

"Change the button background to match the red in our logo (#FF0000)."

Why it works: Clear visual/hex code reference leaves no room for error.

"Swap the order of the text and the image so the text is on the left."

Why it works: Specific layout instruction = Instant fix.

"Replace the first paragraph with: 'We have been serving the area since 1999'."

Why it works: Gives us the exact text to copy and paste.

Handoff

Saving & Submission

Don't Panic: Auto-Save is On

Your changes are saved automatically directly in your browser as you work. If you accidentally close the tab, or need to finish your review tomorrow, simply reopen the exact same link on the same device and browser, and all your images and text will still be there waiting for you.

When you have reviewed every tab and are completely finished, click the "Finalize & Send" button at the top right of the portal.

The Submission Checklist

  • The Validation Blocker: If you forget to select an "Upload Type" from the dropdown or forget to write a specific comment for any image, the portal will flash a red error. It will automatically scroll you back to the exact image you missed and highlight it in red. You cannot submit until all fields are filled.
  • Check Every Tab: Once again, double-check the sidebar to ensure you didn't miss reviewing any specific pages.
  • The Point of No Return: Once you successfully hit Finalize, the portal compresses your entire review into a ZIP file, emails it directly to our engineers, and wipes your local draft clean. Ensure everything is complete before sending!
